Mornings used to be something I rushed through.

Alarm. Phone. Shower. Out the door.
No space to think. No space to feel.

But somewhere along the way, I realized I was starting my days already behind.

So I changed one small thing — I slowed down.

## Less rush, more presence

A slow morning doesn't have to be complicated. It isn't about waking up earlier or having the perfect routine.

It's about giving yourself a little room to breathe before the world asks for your attention.

I've started leaving my phone in another room until after breakfast. I make coffee slowly, watch it drip instead of scrolling while it brews. I sit by the window for a few minutes before anything else happens.

None of it is dramatic. But it changes the shape of the whole day.

## What it gave back to me

The strange thing is, slowing down in the morning hasn't made me less productive. If anything, I move through the rest of the day with a little more clarity, a little less noise in my head.

Maybe that's the real point. Not to do more before nine, but to arrive at nine as someone who's already, quietly, arrived.
